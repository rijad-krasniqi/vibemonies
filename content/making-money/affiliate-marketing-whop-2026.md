## The Three Traffic Sources That Work

You can have the best affiliate offer in the world. Without traffic, it's worthless.

Here are the three traffic sources I've seen work best for Whop affiliate promotions:

### Source 1: SEO Content

This is the long game but it compounds like crazy.

The play:

- Find products on Whop in niches you understand
- Create content targeting keywords people search before buying
- Rank, get traffic, earn commissions

Example keywords that work:

- "[Product name] review"
- "Best [niche] course 2026"
- "[Product name] vs [competitor]"
- "Is [product name] worth it"
- "[Niche] community recommendations"

The beauty of SEO is passive income. Write the content once, rank it, and it sends you commissions for months or years.

### Source 2: Twitter/X Threads

Twitter is where the Whop audience lives. Crypto traders, indie hackers, side hustle seekers - they're all on Twitter.

The play:

- Build an audience in a niche (trading, AI, fitness, whatever)
- Share valuable content consistently
- Occasionally promote relevant Whop products to your audience

The key is relevance. If you're a fitness account promoting a crypto trading course, it won't convert. If you're a trading account promoting a trading signals group, it will.

Authenticity matters too. Don't just shill. Actually use the products you promote. Share real results. People can smell fake recommendations.

### Source 3: YouTube Reviews

Video reviews convert incredibly well. People want to see what they're buying before they buy it.

The play:

- Buy access to products on Whop
- Create honest reviews showing the inside
- Put your affiliate link in the description

Yes, you're investing money upfront. But one good review video can pay for itself 100x over if it ranks for buyer-intent keywords.

The products that work best for video reviews are courses and communities - things where you can actually show what's inside.

## Finding the Right Products to Promote

Not all Whop products are equal. Here's how to find ones worth promoting:

**Check the sales count:** Products with existing sales have proven demand. Avoid products nobody's buying.

**Look at the commission structure:** 50% is great, but recurring 30% might be better long-term. Do the math.

**Evaluate the sales page:** If their sales page is garbage, you'll struggle to convert traffic. Good products have good marketing.

**Check the creator:** Is the creator active? Do they have a following? Products from established creators convert better.

**Test it yourself:** Buy the product. Use it. If it's good, promote it. If it sucks, don't.

## Mistakes to Avoid

**Promoting everything:** Pick 3-5 products you genuinely believe in. Quality over quantity.

**Ignoring recurring products:** One-time commissions are nice but recurring is how you build real income.

**Not disclosing:** Always disclose affiliate relationships. It's legally required and builds trust.

**Choosing products only by commission:** A 50% commission on garbage nobody wants is worse than 30% on something that sells itself.

**Giving up too early:** Affiliate income compounds. Month 1 might be $50. Month 6 might be $5,000. Stick with it.
